Module 6 · Activity 2 of 3

Critical Impacts Self-Assessment

⏱ About 12 minutes

For each of the eight critical impact areas, reflect honestly on what it means for your venture. No impact area is irrelevant — even if it feels distant now.

1. Data & Network Effects
Who controls the data? Is your solution dependent on big platforms?
2. Trust
Can users rely on or understand the AI?
3. Augmentation vs. Automation
Does AI empower or replace humans in our work?
4. Ethics & Algorithms
Is our AI fair, explainable, and accountable?
5. Cultural Bias
Whose values are embedded in the AI tools we use?
6. Decision Making
Which decisions should AI never make for us?
7. Environmental Impact
Is the energy cost of our AI use proportionate to the benefit?
8. Predictability
Does AI reduce our openness to the unexpected?
Module 6 · Activity 3 of 3

Responsible AI Checklist

⏱ About 8 minutes

Before deploying any AI system in a social venture context, these eight questions should be answered. Check each one you can currently answer clearly — and note what you still need to work out.

✓
1. Who benefits — and who bears the risk? I have honestly mapped who gains from our AI use and who carries the risk.
✓
2. Whose data — and on whose terms? I know where our data comes from, who owns it, and the consent conditions.
✓
3. Can it be explained? I can explain to a beneficiary in plain language how the AI made a decision that affected them.
✓
4. Who is accountable when it goes wrong? A specific named person in our team is responsible for the AI system's outputs.
✓
5. Have we tested for bias? The AI has been checked for equitable performance across the communities we serve.
✓
6. Is there a human override? For every consequential AI decision, a human can review, challenge, and override it.
✓
7. What is the environmental cost? We have considered the energy consumption and found it proportionate to the benefit.
✓
8. Are we ready for the responsibility? Moving deeper requires accepting greater responsibility toward our community — we have discussed this as a team.
📝 Which questions can you answer clearly right now — and which do you still need to work out?
⚖️ What is one ethical commitment you will make about how you use or build AI in your work?
Small and specific is better than big and vague
