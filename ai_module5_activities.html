Warm-up — Imagining the Possible

Module 5 asks you to think bigger — beyond what's immediately practical toward what's systemically possible. Let your imagination lead before the framework guides it.

Level 3 Case Analysis

⏱ About 10 minutes

Module 5 describes four types of Level 3 transformative AI. Read each one and analyse which resonates most with the challenge you identified in your warm-up.

📖 Which of the four Level 3 cases resonates most with your field or the challenge you care about?
Community-trained climate/health AI · Multilingual indigenous knowledge archive · Participatory democracy platform · Community co-designed learning system
🔍 What makes this case genuinely "Level 3" — not just Level 1 or 2?
What would be different about the system, the governance, or the impact if it were downgraded to Supportive or Integral?
⚠️ What are the biggest risks of this kind of transformative AI — and who bears those risks?
Module 5 · Activity 3 of 3

Design Your Own Transformative AI System

⏱ About 10 minutes

Use the Level 3 prompt from the module to sketch your own vision. This is speculative — the goal is to stretch your thinking, not to produce a project plan.

The prompt: "If AI were a public good — like roads or libraries — how would you design it to serve your community's biggest needs? Think about: Access (who's left out now?), Equity (what guardrails would prevent harm or bias?), Impact (what stubborn social problem could it help solve?). Constraint: it must be free/open, ethical, and community-controlled."
🚀 Sketch your transformative AI system — what would it do, for whom, and how would it be governed?
🛡️ How would you keep it accountable — and what are the three biggest risks to design against?
🔍 Reflection: What would need to change — in you, your organisation, or the ecosystem — to make something like this possible?
